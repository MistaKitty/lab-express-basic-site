Fernando Pessoa is known for his extensive literary work, which covers various styles and themes. Some of Fernando Pessoa's most important works include:

"Mensagem": A poetic work that is one of the best known and studied in Portuguese literature. "Mensagem" is a celebration of the history, mythology, and identity of Portugal.

"The Book of Disquiet": This is one of Pessoa's most important works, although it was published posthumously. It consists of a collection of fragments, thoughts, and reflections on life, identity, and the human condition, reflecting the complexity and restlessness of the author.

Heteronyms: Pessoa is known for creating various heteronyms, that is, distinct literary personas with their own styles, personalities, and worldviews. Some of the most famous include Alberto Caeiro, Ãlvaro de Campos, and Ricardo Reis. Each of these heteronyms has a unique poetic work, reflecting different perspectives and life philosophies.

Individual poems: In addition to the main works, Pessoa wrote a multitude of individual poems that address a wide range of themes, from metaphysical and philosophical issues to reflections on everyday life and the human experience.

These are just some of Fernando Pessoa's most prominent works, which demonstrate his genius and versatility as a writer. His work continues to be studied, appreciated, and reinterpreted by readers and scholars around the world.
